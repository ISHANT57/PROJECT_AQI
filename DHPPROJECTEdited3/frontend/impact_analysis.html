Understanding Air Quality Health Impacts

Air pollution is one of the world's largest health and environmental problems. Research has linked air pollution to various health conditions, including respiratory diseases, cardiovascular problems, and even reduced cognitive function. Long-term exposure to elevated levels of air pollution can significantly impact life expectancy and quality of life.

This analysis tool helps you understand how different levels of air pollution affect human health across various age groups and provides insights into the correlation between air quality and public health metrics.

Health Effects by Pollutant Type

Different pollutants affect human health in various ways

PM2.5

Fine Particulate Matter

Tiny particles that can penetrate deep into lungs and even enter the bloodstream.

Short-term Effects:
  • Irritation of eyes, nose, and throat
  • Coughing, chest tightness, and shortness of breath
  • Irregular heartbeat and reduced lung function
Long-term Effects:
  • Respiratory infections and asthma
  • Chronic bronchitis and emphysema
  • Heart disease and cardiovascular problems
  • Reduced cognitive function

O3

Ground-level Ozone

A reactive gas that forms when pollutants from cars and industry react in sunlight.

Short-term Effects:
  • Coughing and throat irritation
  • Chest pain and difficulty breathing
  • Reduced exercise capacity
Long-term Effects:
  • Inflammation of lung tissue
  • Permanent lung damage
  • Low birth weight in newborns
  • Premature mortality

NO2

Nitrogen Dioxide

A gaseous air pollutant produced primarily during the combustion of fossil fuels.

Short-term Effects:
  • Eye, nose, and throat irritation
  • Respiratory symptoms like wheezing and coughing
  • Increased respiratory infections
Long-term Effects:
  • Asthma development and exacerbation
  • Reduced lung function growth in children
  • Greater susceptibility to respiratory infections
  • Cardiovascular effects

Mitigating Health Risks

Actions you can take to protect yourself from air pollution

Indoor Air Quality
  • Use air purifiers with HEPA filters
  • Keep windows closed on high pollution days
  • Use kitchen exhaust fans when cooking
  • Avoid smoking indoors
  • Maintain ventilation systems
Personal Protection
  • Wear N95/N99 masks on polluted days
  • Use air quality apps for alerts
  • Avoid outdoor exercise during high pollution
  • Stay hydrated to help flush toxins
  • Take omega-3 supplements
Diet & Nutrition
  • Eat antioxidant-rich foods
  • Increase vitamin C and E intake
  • Consume anti-inflammatory foods
  • Maintain adequate vitamin D levels
  • Stay well-hydrated
Transportation Choices
  • Use public transportation
  • Consider electric vehicles
  • Bike or walk for short trips
  • Keep car windows closed in traffic
  • Use cabin air filters in vehicles
