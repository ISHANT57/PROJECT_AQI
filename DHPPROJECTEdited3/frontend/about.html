About Our Air Quality Platform

Learn about our mission to provide comprehensive air quality data and raise awareness about global air pollution.

Our Mission

The Global Air Quality Monitoring Platform is dedicated to providing accurate, up-to-date information about air quality around the world. Our mission is to:

  • Raise awareness about the global air pollution crisis and its health impacts
  • Make air quality data accessible and understandable to everyone
  • Empower individuals and communities to take action to improve air quality
  • Support policymakers and researchers with comprehensive air pollution data
  • Track progress in air quality improvement efforts worldwide

We believe that access to information is the first step toward meaningful change. By providing transparent air quality data, we hope to contribute to a cleaner, healthier future for all.

IQAir provides real-time air quality data from thousands of monitoring stations worldwide. We use their data to populate our interactive map and track the most polluted cities globally.

Understanding Air Quality Index (AQI)

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a standardized indicator developed by the US Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) to communicate how clean or polluted the air is and what associated health effects might be a concern.

AQI Range Category Color Health Implications
0-50 Good Good Air quality is satisfactory, and air pollution poses little or no risk.
51-100 Moderate Moderate Air quality is acceptable. However, there may be a risk for some people, particularly those who are unusually sensitive to air pollution.
101-150 Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups USG Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects. The general public is less likely to be affected.
151-200 Unhealthy Unhealthy Everyone may begin to experience health effects. Members of sensitive groups may experience more serious health effects.
201-300 Very Unhealthy Very Unhealthy Health alert: The risk of health effects is increased for everyone.
301+ Hazardous Hazardous Health warning of emergency conditions: everyone is more likely to be affected.
Note: Different countries may use variations of the AQI with different scales and breakpoints. Our platform primarily uses the US EPA standard for consistency.

Frequently Asked Questions

Our air quality data is updated regularly throughout the day. The exact frequency depends on the data source, but most locations are updated at least once every hour. For real-time critical information, we recommend checking our interactive map.

Differences in AQI values between sources can occur for several reasons: different measurement methods, different calculation formulas (as some countries use their own AQI calculation methods), different monitoring station locations, and timing differences in data updates. We attempt to standardize our data where possible for consistency.

On days with poor air quality, you can: limit outdoor activities (especially strenuous ones), stay indoors with windows closed, use air purifiers with HEPA filters, wear N95 masks when outdoors, avoid high-traffic areas, and check our website for real-time updates on air quality in your area.
